The Lion

Male lions can be 10 ft long, stand 3.5–4 ft tall at the shoulder, and weigh 330–550 lbs. Females are slightly smaller, at 9 ft long, 265–395 lbs, and shorter and more slender than males.Lions have long bodies, short legs, sharp claws, and large heads.They are covered in yellow-brown hair, and males have a coarse mane on their head, neck, and shoulders. Lions are the only cats that live in groups called prides. Prides hunt, raise cubs, defend their territory, and defend against other lions. Females are the primary hunters in a pride. In the wild, male lions typically live 10–12 years, while females can live up to 14–15 years. In captivity, lions can live 20–25 years. Panthera leo.Big cats, which also include tigers, leopards, and jaguars. Lions and humans shared a common ancestor between 98,000 and 52,000 years ago.

The Cheetah

Cheetahs are the fastest land animal on Earth, reaching speeds of up to 70 miles per hour in just three seconds.Cheetahs have tawny coats with black spots that are unique to each individual, like human fingerprints. They also have black tear lines that run from the inner corners of their eyes to the sides of their mouths. Adult cheetahs are 3.7 to 4.6 feet long, with a tail that is 2 to 2.7 feet long. They weigh between 77 and 143 pounds, with males being slightly larger than females. Cheetahs hunt small to medium-sized prey, such as springbok, gazelle, and impala. They stalk their prey from a distance of 200 to 330 feet, then charge, trip, and bite the throat to kill. Cheetahs live in three main social groups: females and their cubs, male coalitions, and solitary males. Male coalitions are made up of two or three males, often brothers, who live and hunt together. Cheetahs live an average of up to 14 years in the wild, and up to 20 years in captivity. Cheetahs are threatened by larger predators, such as lions, leopards, wild dogs, and hyenas. Cheetahs live in a variety of habitats, including savannahs, arid mountain ranges, and hilly desert terrain. The cheetah is the world's fastest land animal and Africa's most endangered big cat.

The Bear

Bears are mammals that belong to the family Ursidae. They can be as small as four feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). They're found throughout North America, South America, Europe, and Asia. Bears are mammals that belong to the family Ursidae. These animals are widespread, with members living on both the hemispheres. Bears are predominantly carnivores, but also consume fruits and leaves. Some bears, such as pandas consume bamboo shoots, which makes up 99% of its diet. Or plural bear : any of a family (Ursidae of the order Carnivora) of large heavy mammals of America and Eurasia that have long shaggy hair, rudimentary tails, and plantigrade feet and feed largely on fruit, plant matter, and insects as well as on flesh. The Tiger

Tigers are powerful hunters with sharp teeth, strong jaws and agile bodies. They are the largest terrestrial mammal whose diet consists entirely of meat; the largest tiger ever recorded was an Amur tiger.The tiger's closest relative is the lion. In fact, without fur, it is difficult to distinguish a tiger from a lion.Tigers are the largest of all cats and are elusive creatures. It is known for its ability to leap, swim, and spring. It also has many other features like a long tail with black stripes and an orange coat. Tigers are also found in Nepal, where they have been considered sacred animals.
